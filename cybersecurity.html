    Famous People in Cybersecurity

    Throughout history, many individuals have made significant contributions to the field of cybersecurity. These pioneers have shaped the way we protect digital information and secure our online world.

    Kevin Mitnick
    Former Hacker & Security Consultant

    One of the most famous hackers in history, turned security consultant. Known for his social engineering skills and later became a respected cybersecurity expert, author, and speaker.

    Bruce Schneier
    Cryptographer & Security Expert

    Renowned cryptographer, security technologist, and author. Known for his influential books on cryptography and security, and his work on cryptographic algorithms and security protocols.

    Eugene Kaspersky
    Founder of Kaspersky Lab

    Co-founder of Kaspersky Lab, one of the world's largest cybersecurity companies. Expert in malware research and antivirus technology, known for discovering and analyzing major cyber threats.

    Mikko Hyppönen
    Chief Research Officer at F-Secure

    Finnish cybersecurity expert and researcher. Known for his work in malware research, computer security, and his popular TED talks on cybersecurity and privacy.

    Whitfield Diffie
    Cryptographer & Inventor

    Co-inventor of public-key cryptography and the Diffie-Hellman key exchange. His work revolutionized modern cryptography and enabled secure communications over the internet.

    Adi Shamir
    Cryptographer & Turing Award Winner

    Israeli cryptographer and one of the inventors of the RSA algorithm. Winner of the Turing Award for his contributions to cryptography and computer security.

    Cybersecurity Program Overview

    TalTech offers comprehensive cybersecurity education at both Bachelor's and Master's levels, preparing students for careers in information security, digital forensics, and secure systems development.

    Bachelor's in Cybersecurity

    The Bachelor's program provides fundamental knowledge in information security, covering network security, cryptography, secure programming, and security management. Students learn to identify vulnerabilities, implement security measures, and respond to cyber threats.

    Master's in Cybersecurity

    The Master's program offers advanced specialization in secure systems design, digital forensics, and penetration testing. Students work on cutting-edge research projects and collaborate with Estonian cybersecurity companies.

    Research Opportunities

    TalTech's cybersecurity research focuses on cryptographic protocols, secure multi-party computation, privacy-preserving technologies, and blockchain security. Students can participate in research projects with industry partners and contribute to cutting-edge security solutions.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Below you'll find answers to the most common questions about the cybersecurity programs at TalTech.

    • What are the admission requirements for the cybersecurity program?

      For Bachelor's programs, you need a secondary education diploma with good grades in mathematics and physics. For Master's programs, a Bachelor's degree in computer science, IT, or related field is required. International students should provide proof of English proficiency (IELTS, TOEFL, or equivalent).

    • What career opportunities are available after graduation?

      Graduates work as security analysts, penetration testers, security engineers, digital forensics specialists, and security consultants. Many find positions at Estonian companies like Guardtime, Cybernetica, Nortal, and international technology firms. Government agencies and cybersecurity firms also actively recruit TalTech graduates.

    • What makes TalTech's cybersecurity program unique?

      Estonia's experience as a digitally advanced nation provides unique learning opportunities. TalTech collaborates closely with Estonian e-government initiatives and leading cybersecurity companies. Students gain hands-on experience with real-world security challenges and benefit from Estonia's strong cybersecurity ecosystem.

    • Are there opportunities for international students?

      Yes! TalTech welcomes international students and offers programs in English. The university provides support services for international students, including housing assistance, orientation programs, and language courses. Estonia's multicultural environment makes it an ideal place for international students to study cybersecurity.

    • What practical skills will I gain from the program?

      Students learn practical skills in penetration testing, vulnerability assessment, secure coding, digital forensics, incident response, and security architecture design. The program includes hands-on labs, real-world projects, and internships with industry partners, ensuring graduates are job-ready upon completion.
